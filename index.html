Hi, there!

March 9, 2020 TAGS :about-me:

Using these pages to document the loot I've collected on my adventures and expeditions. What they will be, that is too early

10 Things I Love

  1. My kids to bits and Pia, their mother, because it is the only thing that matters.
  2. Data and statistics because of the beauty they behold.
  3. Emacs and keyboard mastery because they reflect the lost art of steep learning curve UX. Although, I'm not entirely certain.
  4. Slide show presentations. For far too long, slide show presentations have they been misunderstood and abused.
  5. Jokes, much like unexpected novel ideas, art or philosophy. Essentially the same thing.
  6. Nonfiction, including fiction, is a factual representation of our inner existence.
  7. Lisp programming because of its minimal aesthetics and ease of typing.
  8. 8-bit computers, as such is my age.
  9. Annoying music as non-annoying music drives me mad.
  10. Borges, as also he was a friend of recursion.

10 More Things I Enjoy

  1. Emotions, they are 70% of what we actually mean
  2. Multiple perspectives, otherwise all would be flat
  3. Journalism, as it can, in fact, also be used for good
  4. Systems, be they techical, ecological, psycological or artistic, they are as attractive as they are brittle
  5. Maps in the wi(l)dest sense of the wo(l)rd
  6. Looking people in the eyes regularily as it is the only way we can feel what they feel
  7. Randomness, it helps us discover more interesting patterns
  8. Hacking teams and groups of people because when it succeeds it can bring so much joy to all involved
  9. Youtube, for it offers all the lectures we will ever need
  10. Reading and writing lists, as lists, one suspects, are the true reason we made the leap and started to read and write; to remember, not to express

The Design Bio For Corporate Use

September 30, 2021 TAGS :about-me:

Sami Kallinen has actively used service design and design thinking tools for product development, capability development, and organizational development since the late 2000s, in addition to having built professional digital products since the early 1990s and having led and built the digital product capacity of some of the major Finnish brands. He likes to state that he enjoys hacking and growing communities, whether it's for a commercial organization, an anarchistically organised cultural center in Amsterdam, or film festivals and communities in Stockholm, not to mention 8-bit-sheep where he currently is active in.

He also has a technical and data science background, having written code since the early 1980s and working with data for the past ten years.

Moreover, he worked as an active exhibiting artist in the fields of media art and online art in Amsterdam before returning to Finland in 2008, displaying his work in festivals and galleries such as De Appel in Amsterdam, Taidehalli in Helsinki, and Centre Pompidou in Paris. He's recently created some work that straddles the line between generative art and AI art using generative adversarial networks.

He has also sometimes been heard humblesheepbragging that he speaks four languages equally badly: Swedish, Finnish, English and Baa.

The 'D' in the CDO refers to Digital, Data, and Design in this case of Sami.

Facing Grief

The Weeping Gallery, Sami Kallinen's video installation until 7.1. at the Taidehalli gallery (Nervanderinkatu 3). Tue-Fri 11-18, Wed 11-20, Sat-Sun 12-17.

December 12, 2006, TAGS :art:video-installation:net-art:review:

Uimonen Anu, Helsingin Sanomat

"VISUAL ARTS. Amsterdam-based artist Sami Kallinen (b. 1972) grabbed a video camera while his mother was lying in Turku, 1389 kilometers away, struggling with a serious illness. He decided to shoot a short clip of the view from his home window every day.

Autumn trees photographed from a window are combined with pictures of people grieving in Kallinen's two-part video installation in the Taidehalli gallery. However, because the images are on opposing walls of the area, you must choose which to look at. There are two ways to face grief.

Autumn trees photographed from a window are combined with pictures of people grieving in Kallinen's two-part video installation in the Taidehalli gallery. However, because the images are on opposing walls of the area, you must choose which to look at. There are two ways to face grief.

You can take part in the crying yourself: Kallinen collects videos of real crying situations at www.weepingallery.net, from where they are sent to the exhibition.

We are not used to looking at the open expression of grief from strangers. It can be experienced in different ways, embarrassing or liberating, depending on your own emotional state. Crying can also be a unifying experience, as weeping women or crying walls say.

However, the days of anxiety depicted in the window, accompanied by music played by Kallinen, dominate this whole. The slight unevenness of the window glass softens the image and makes the everyday outside world feel unreal and unattainable. Such is the world in the midst of great sorrow."
